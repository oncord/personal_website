About Richard

I'm a dual-enrolled student studying Computer Science and Mathematics at WashU & William Jewell College. My academic journey began at William Jewell College, where I discovered my passion for computation and technical innovation between math curriculum.

After establishing a strong foundation at William Jewell, I transferred to Washington University in St. Louis to pursue more advanced coursework and opportunities related to Computer Science. This transition allowed me to dive deeper into software engineering principles, low-level systems programming, and hardware-software integrations along with a dab of machine learning. My coursework challenged me to think critically about system design, optimization, and scalable solutions.

Throughout my undergraduate experience, I've worked on diverse projects ranging from bare-metal STM32 firmware development in C to full-stack web applications being deployed to AWS. This breadth of experience has taught me to adapt quickly to new technologies and frameworks while maintaining a focus on writing clean, efficient, and maintainable code.

As I approach graduation, I'm excited to transition into industry where I can apply my academic knowledge to real-world problems, contribute to innovative projects, and continue growing as a software engineer.
